Maintenance Tips for High-Mileage Drivers

  • Change oil more frequently High-mileage driving means more engine wear. Consider oil changes every 3,000-5,000 miles instead of the standard 7,500.
  • Rotate tires regularly With city driving and frequent stops, your tires wear unevenly. Rotate every 5,000-6,000 miles to extend tire life.
  • Monitor brake pads closely Stop-and-go traffic wears brakes faster. Check them every 10,000 miles or sooner if you hear squeaking.
  • Keep your car clean Passenger ratings depend on cleanliness. Regular detailing protects your seats and maintains that 5-star rating.
  • Don't ignore warning lights A check engine light can mean anything from a loose gas cap to a serious problem. Get it diagnosed quickly.
